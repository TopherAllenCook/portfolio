# Harvest Vintages — TBF's vintage journal
*The "wine of cannabis" data layer. Agent reads this for vintage release content, terroir storytelling, and timeline claims.*

## Pre-TBF lineage
- **2003 — Garden of Eden launches.** One of the earliest licensed cannabis retailers in California. The lineage's start.
- **2011 — Analytical lab built.** Initial focus: potency and patient safety for terminally ill and immunocompromised patients.
- **2013 — Cannabis vapes emerge.** Garden of Eden enters extraction and hardware. Cartomizer-era.
- **2016 — Terpene focus.** Naming conventions enable some of the earliest principal component analysis of cannabis flower. Internal software platform later becomes Treez.io.
- **2018 — Pivot pressure.** Regulatory changes force pulling back on farming; vape products suffer. Cannabis-derived terpene supply collapses across the industry.

## TBF era

### 2019 — Founding (Season I)
- California regulates hemp farming under the 2018 Farm Bill.
- Terpene Belt Farms founded as the solution to the universal cannabis-derived terpene supply problem.
- Pilot program established.
- **Narrative:** "Turns out, the lack of high quality cannabis derived terpenes was not just an Eden problem. It was a universal issue and we set out to solve it."

### 2020 — Scaled pilot (Season II)
- Farming footprint: **160 acres**.
- On-site processing system built: field-to-oil in **90 minutes**.
- Process branded: **Fresh Never Frozen®**.
- **Narrative:** Proved world-class oils can be produced under the hemp framework.

### 2021 — Brand-partner traction (Season III)
- Farming footprint: **240 acres**.
- Second harvest team added.
- Throughput at peak: **5 tons/hour**.
- **Narrative:** "Rave reviews coming from our brand partners."

### 2022 — Consistency focus (Season IV)
- Variety count: **22**.
- New terpinolene-dominant exotic-category varieties.
- Never-before-seen minor pairings.
- **Narrative:** "Realigned our focus on consistency."

### 2023 — The stride year (Season V)
- "Five years and we finally hit our stride."
- Yields vastly exceeded forecasts.
- Fruit-forward, pinene-dominant exotic-category varieties.
- **Narrative:** "Head and shoulders better than what we have seen in the market."

### 2024 — Dessert outlook (Season VI)
- "Candies dominating in the flower market" → dessert-leaning vintage.
- Fast start, slowed by a long heatwave.
- Late ripeners with no early rains.
- **Narrative:** Set up to chase Season V's yield bar.
- **Regulatory milestone:** First FDA GRAS application submitted (alcoholic and non-alcoholic beverages).

### 2025 — UCLA grant year
- Pine-129 vintage at 8.2% caryophyllene (sample data; verify against actual COA).
- **Strategic shift:** Nexus Agriscience selected for UCLA-led California state-funded cannabis research grant.
- TBF terpene oils become research input dataset.
- **Implication:** First peer-reviewed research on full-spectrum native cannabis terpene behavior in product matrices.

## Vintage-story narrative scaffolding (for content)
When a new vintage releases, structure the announcement as:
1. **The number.** One specific quantitative claim (e.g., "8.2% caryophyllene").
2. **The season story.** What the weather, harvest, or operational change in that season produced.
3. **The formulation implication.** What that vintage is good for (vape vs beverage vs edible) and why, by chemistry.
4. **The wine-of-cannabis frame.** Tie back to vintage/terroir/seasonal-craft positioning.
5. **The soft CTA.** Sample pack or technical conversation.
