# Research Corpus — the citations TBF stands on
*Every TBF blog closes with a Sources Cited block. These six papers form the citation backbone. The agent uses this file when generating any technical commentary so the citation pattern stays consistent across content.*

## Core six (cited repeatedly in TBF's existing blogs)

### 1. Russo 2011 — The entourage-effect foundation
> Russo EB. Taming THC: potential cannabis synergy and phytocannabinoid-terpenoid entourage effects. *British Journal of Pharmacology*. 2011;163(7):1344-1364.

Use when: discussing entourage effect, cannabinoid-terpenoid interaction, full-spectrum vs. isolate.

### 2. Gertsch et al. 2008 — β-caryophyllene as dietary cannabinoid
> Gertsch, J., et al. (2008). Beta-caryophyllene is a dietary cannabinoid. *Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences*, 105(26), 9099–9104.

Use when: discussing β-caryophyllene, CB2 receptor binding, anti-inflammatory positioning.

### 3. Harada et al. 2018 — Linalool anxiolysis
> Harada H, Kashiwadani H, Kanmura Y, Kuwaki T. (2018). Linalool odor-induced anxiolytic effects in mice. *Frontiers in Behavioral Neuroscience*, 12.

Use when: discussing linalool, calming applications, beverage wellness positioning.

### 4. Sae-Tang et al. 2024 — Light intensity and terpene yield
> Sae-Tang W, et al. (2024). High light intensity improves yield of specialized metabolites in medicinal cannabis (Cannabis sativa L.). *Journal of Applied Research on Medicinal and Aromatic Plants*, 43, 100583.

Use when: discussing cultivation factors, vintage variation, terroir.

### 5. Surendran et al. 2021 — Myrcene health benefits
> Surendran S, et al. (2021). Myrcene — What are the potential health benefits of this flavouring and aroma agent? *Frontiers in Nutrition*, 8.

Use when: discussing myrcene, sedation profile, beverage and edibles applications.

### 6. Russo 2019 — The case for full-spectrum
> Russo EB. (2019). The case for the entourage effect and conventional breeding of clinical cannabis: No "strain," no gain. *Frontiers in Plant Science*, 9. doi.org/10.3389/fpls.2018.01969

Use when: positioning against the THC-percentage-only narrative; cultivar vs strain framing.

## How the agent uses this file
When generating any technical content, the agent:
1. Identifies the claim being made (e.g., "β-caryophyllene is thermally stable up to 208°C")
2. Selects the citation that supports it (Gertsch et al. 2008 for β-caryophyllene chemistry context)
3. Inserts an inline cite link to the DOI or PMC URL
4. Adds the full citation to a "Sources Cited" block at the end of the piece

This maintains the citation density TBF has already established as their authority signal.

## What's notably absent (potential gap to fill)
TBF cites no internal TBF proprietary research yet — all peer-reviewed papers are from third parties. If TBF publishes its own white papers (e.g., from the UCLA grant work), those become the highest-trust citation source.
