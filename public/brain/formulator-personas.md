# Formulator Personas — TBF's actual readers
*Four personas. Every piece of content should be tagged with the primary persona it's addressing. The agent uses these to choose vocabulary and structure.*

---

## Persona 1 — "Vape Cart R&D Lead"
**Title patterns:** Director of R&D, Senior Formulation Scientist, Head of Vape Product
**Company stage:** Series A through public-cannabis cap (large MSO labs and mid-market vape brands)
**Day-to-day:** Spec'ing terpene blends for new SKUs. Running stability studies. Negotiating with hardware vendors. Defending product specs to compliance.

**Pain points TBF can solve:**
- Cart hardware compatibility (clogging, leaching, polymer interaction)
- Thermal stability across the 180-230°C vape window
- Batch-to-batch flavor consistency under accelerated aging
- Differentiating against commodity botanical blends without paying a 5× premium

**Vocabulary they use:**
viscosity (cP), 510-thread, ceramic core, flash point, accelerated aging, GC-MS, single-cell vs. dual-cell hardware, leaching, off-flavor

**Surface preferences:**
- LinkedIn (where they read peer content)
- Trade press (MJBizDaily, Cannabis Business Times)
- B2B email (technical, no fluff)
- Conference floor (MJBizCon, Hall of Flowers)

**What converts them:** Specific data — temperatures, cP values, COA percentages, hardware compatibility matrices.

---

## Persona 2 — "Beverage Formulator"
**Title patterns:** Product Development Manager, R&D Manager, Beverage Innovation Lead
**Company stage:** Mid-market CBD/THC beverage brands, RTD startups, larger CPG R&D departments exploring hemp ingredients
**Day-to-day:** Solubility, emulsion stability, shelf-life under variable temperatures, flavor masking, label-claim defensibility.

**Pain points TBF can solve:**
- Terpene solubility in water-based systems
- Emulsion stability across the cold chain
- Light-degradation in clear bottles
- Achieving authentic cannabis flavor under FDA-compliant formulation constraints

**Vocabulary they use:**
emulsification, nano-emulsion, particle size (nm), pH stability, nitrogen blanketing, accelerated shelf-life testing, organoleptic, GRAS

**Surface preferences:**
- LinkedIn + industry-specific publications (BevNet, Drink Beat)
- Conference floor (BevNet Live, NACS, IFT)
- Long-form email with linked PDFs (technical attachments)

**What converts them:** Pre-emulsified samples + a stability dataset. Concentration guidance in the 0.005-0.05% range. FDA GRAS positioning.

---

## Persona 3 — "Edible / Confectionery Formulator"
**Title patterns:** Senior Food Scientist, Edibles Production Manager, R&D Chef
**Company stage:** Established edible brands (Wana, Kiva, Wyld category) and CPG cannabis newcomers
**Day-to-day:** Recipe development, heat-stability under cooking, food-matrix interaction, dose accuracy, allergen-free claims, scaling production.

**Pain points TBF can solve:**
- Terpene survival under baking / heating processes
- Lipid-system terpene retention
- Avoiding bitterness or off-notes at higher concentrations
- Strain-authentic flavor without botanical-blend "approximation"

**Vocabulary they use:**
microencapsulation, lipid-carrier, matrix interaction, organoleptic testing, hot fill, cold-process, sous vide infusion

**Surface preferences:**
- LinkedIn + culinary trade press (Food Business News)
- Long-form blog content (they research extensively)
- Sample-pack-first: they want to taste before they call

**What converts them:** Cold-process delivery options. Lipid-carrier compatibility data. Sample kits in bulk-edible-friendly concentrations.

---

## Persona 4 — "Distributor / Co-Packer / Procurement"
**Title patterns:** VP Procurement, Director of Sourcing, Co-packing Manager, Brand Owner (smaller)
**Company stage:** Multi-brand distribution, white-label co-packers, owner-operators
**Day-to-day:** Sourcing inputs at scale across multiple SKUs. Quality and compliance documentation. COA review. Vendor consolidation.

**Pain points TBF can solve:**
- Single supplier across multiple aroma families and price tiers
- Documented compliance (COA, SDS, GRAS-track positioning)
- Consistent supply across vintages (the blending / "house profile" strategy)
- White-label-ready bulk pricing

**Vocabulary they use:**
COA, SDS, lot consistency, MOQ, white-label, co-pack, audit trail, supplier qualification, lab accreditation

**Surface preferences:**
- Trade-show booth conversations
- Direct sales outreach (cold email, intro calls)
- LinkedIn + cannabis-industry conferences
- PDF spec sheets and price lists, not blog content

**What converts them:** Vendor qualification documentation. Multi-year supply assurance. Clear pricing tiers. "Wholesale program" framing.

---

## How the agent uses this file
When generating content for any surface, the agent picks the primary persona based on the topic:
- "Vape thermal stability" → Vape Cart R&D Lead → use cP, 510-thread, ceramic core vocabulary
- "Beverage launch" → Beverage Formulator → use solubility, emulsion, GRAS vocabulary
- "Vintage release" → Distributor → use consistency, supply assurance, MOQ vocabulary
- "Edible formulation" → Edible Formulator → use lipid-carrier, microencapsulation vocabulary

The output is then tagged with persona in the brief so the human reviewer knows who it was written for.
