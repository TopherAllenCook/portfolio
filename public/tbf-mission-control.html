01 The Brain

One brain.
Every agent reads from it.

The hard part of marketing automation isn't the wiring — it's the voice. We solved that once. Ten markdown files hold everything TBF knows about itself: how you write, what you grow, which vintage hit 8.2% caryophyllene, who your formulators are, and what they search for. Every agent loads this brain before it writes a single word. Build the brain once; all nine automations inherit it.

The loop every agent runs
Trigger
A webhook, cron, RSS poll, or store event fires — form submit, new COA, new blog, Monday 9am.
Load the brain
Reads voice-manual.md plus the relevant context file for this job. Same scaffold, every time.
Agent · Claude
Claude Sonnet 4.6 generates with a structured-output parser — clean JSON, never a wall of text to untangle.
Human-in-loop
Lands in an Airtable / Slack / email approval queue. Nothing publishes without a person clicking yes.
Publish / send
Ships to the surface: social, inbox, the storefront, a Google Doc, a PDP.

Why this matters: because all nine agents share one loop and one brain, a tenth automation isn't a rebuild — it's a new trigger and a new output sink. By month three the brain has every blog, every COA, every competitor brief embedded. New automations then ship in hours, not weeks.
